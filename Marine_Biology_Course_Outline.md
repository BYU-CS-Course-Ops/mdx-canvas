# Introduction to Marine Biology

### A 2-Week Intensive Course Outline

---

**Duration:** 10 instructional days (2 weeks, Monday through Friday)
**Format:** Lecture, lab, and field observation
**Prerequisites:** None. Open to all levels of learners.

---

## Course Description

This two-week intensive course provides a broad introduction to the science of marine biology. Students will explore ocean physics and chemistry, planktonic and benthic communities, coral reef and deep-sea ecosystems, and the biology of marine megafauna. The course concludes with a focus on human impacts and the future of ocean conservation. Each day combines lecture content with hands-on activities, case studies, or virtual field work.

---

# Week 1

---

## Unit 1: Foundations of the Ocean

| Day | Topic | Key Content |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| **Day 1** | **Welcome to the Sea** | Course overview and expectations · History of marine biology as a discipline · Why the ocean matters: ecological and economic significance |
| **Day 2** | **Ocean Geography** | Major ocean basins and their features · Tectonic plates, mid-ocean ridges, and trenches · Mapping the seafloor: bathymetry and sonar technology |
| **Day 3** | **Seawater Chemistry** | Salinity, temperature, and density relationships · Dissolved gases and nutrient cycling · Ocean acidification: causes and consequences |

---

## Unit 2: Life in the Open Water

| Day | Topic | Key Content |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| **Day 4** | **Phytoplankton & Primary Production** | Photosynthetic microorganisms and their global role · Measuring chlorophyll and productivity · Seasonal blooms and dead zones |
| **Day 5** | **Zooplankton & Food Webs** | Diversity of planktonic animals · Trophic levels and energy transfer · The biological pump: carbon sequestration in the deep sea |

---

# Week 2

---

## Unit 3: Coastal & Benthic Ecosystems

| Day | Topic | Key Content |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| **Day 6** | **Coral Reefs** | Coral biology: symbiosis with zooxanthellae · Reef structure, zonation, and biodiversity · Bleaching events and restoration efforts |
| **Day 7** | **Estuaries & Mangroves** | Brackish water dynamics and tidal influence · Mangrove adaptations and nursery habitat functions · Saltmarsh ecology and coastal protection |
| **Day 8** | **The Deep Sea** | Hydrothermal vents and chemosynthesis · Abyssal plain communities and adaptations to pressure · Bioluminescence and deep-sea gigantism |

---

## Unit 4: Marine Megafauna & Conservation

| Day | Topic | Key Content |
|-----|-------|-------------|
| **Day 9** | **Marine Mammals** | Cetacean biology: whales, dolphins, and porpoises · Pinnipeds and sea otters: thermoregulation strategies · Echolocation, migration, and social behavior |
| **Day 10** | **Sharks, Rays & Sea Turtles** | Elasmobranch sensory systems and electroreception · Sea turtle nesting ecology and navigation · Bycatch, finning, and conservation policy |

---

## Assessment & Deliverables

| Component | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| **Daily Participation** | 15% | Engagement in lectures, labs, and discussions |
| **Lab Notebook** | 25% | Observations, data, and reflections from each session |
| **Midweek Quiz (Day 5)** | 15% | Short-answer quiz covering Units 1 and 2 |
| **Group Presentation (Day 9)** | 20% | Team-based deep-dive on a chosen marine ecosystem |
| **Final Reflection Essay (Day 10)** | 25% | 1,500-word essay connecting course themes to a real-world issue |
